GI Physiology Map

Read This First

This assignment is focused on physiology, but you cannot separate structure from function.

You are still responsible for knowing the GI anatomy. The brush border doesn't mean anything if you can't say where it is. Pancreatic enzymes don't work without ducts. Bile is useless without a path to the duodenum. The physiology lives on top of the anatomy. Study both.

What You Will Do

You will build a single-page concept map of GI physiology that organizes mechanical digestion, chemical digestion, secretion, motility, absorption, and hormonal regulation across the length of the GI tract. The goal is integration on one page. By the end you should be able to point to any region and say what is being digested, secreted, absorbed, and what hormones are pulling the strings.

Drawings carry the mechanism. A label without a process, or a process without a location, will not earn full credit.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this activity, you should be able to:

  1. Identify where mechanical digestion happens, by what mechanism, and what triggers it.
  2. Identify where each major digestive enzyme is secreted, what it acts on, and what activates it.
  3. Trace the location, mechanism, and regulation of carbohydrate, protein, lipid, water, and electrolyte absorption.
  4. Map the major GI hormones (gastrin, secretin, CCK, GIP, motilin) to their stimulus, source, target, and effect.
  5. Connect motility patterns (peristalsis, segmentation, mass movement, MMC) to the regions where they occur.

Instructions

  1. Plan before you draw. One page fills up fast. Sketch the GI tract outline lightly, then plan where each system of annotations will go before committing.
  2. Draw the GI tract as a simple anatomical outline, oral cavity to anal canal. Label the major regions (mouth, esophagus, stomach, duodenum, jejunum, ileum, large intestine).
  3. Add accessory organs: salivary glands, liver, gallbladder, pancreas. Show their secretions reaching the GI tract.
  4. Annotate digestion at each region: what is mechanically broken down, what enzyme acts there, what is the substrate, what is the product.
  5. Annotate secretion: where each enzyme, hormone, mucus, HCl, bicarbonate, and bile component originates.
  6. Annotate absorption: what gets absorbed where, by what transport mechanism (active, facilitated, simple diffusion, osmosis).
  7. Map the hormones: gastrin, secretin, CCK, GIP, motilin. Show stimulus, source, target, effect.
  8. Show motility patterns: peristalsis, segmentation, mass movement, migrating motor complex. Indicate where each occurs.
  9. Submit a clear photo or scan of your completed one-page map. Make sure all labels are legible.

Submission Requirements

  • Photo or scan, clear and well-lit, all labels readable.
